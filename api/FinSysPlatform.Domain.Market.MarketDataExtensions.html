InferTradeAction(MarketData, double)

Analyzes the price relative to the market data and returns the result as TradeAction If the tradePrice is less than the bid price, then the result is Sell If the tradePrice is greater than the ask price, then the result is Buy If the tradePrice is between the bid and ask prices, then the result is calculated based on the distance to the bid and ask prices, which is closer

Determining whether a trade was a buy or sell from the price and bid-ask data typically involves comparing the trade price to the prevailing bid and ask prices at the time of the trade.Hereâ€™s how you can generally interpret it: If the trade price equals (or greater) the ask price: It suggests a buy order was executed. This is because buyers typically have to meet the price sellers are asking to make a purchase, especially in a liquid market. If the trade price equals (or less) the bid price: It suggests a sell order was executed.Sellers typically have to match the highest price buyers are currently willing to pay when they want to sell. If the trade price is between the bid and ask: the calculation method is based on the assumption that a buyer or seller is willing to concede some ground from the best available price to execute the transaction more quickly. Here's how you can apply this method:

  • Closer to Ask Price: If the trade price is closer to the ask than the bid, it suggests that the buyer is willing to pay a little more than the highest bid to ensure the purchase is made, making it likely a buy.
  • Closer to Bid Price: Conversely, if the trade price is closer to the bid than the ask, it indicates that the seller is willing to accept a little less than the lowest asking price to execute the sale quickly, suggesting it's likely a sell.
